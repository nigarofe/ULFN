- Act as an expert educational psychometrician and test developer. Your task is to generate a higher-order multiple-choice question (MCQ) for the **SUBJECT** at the academic level of **LEVEL**.
- Please adhere to the following strict guidelines for constructing the question:

# Cognitive Level
- Target the "Apply," "Analyze," or "Evaluate" level of the revised Bloom's Taxonomy. The question must require multilogical thinking, meaning the student must synthesize multiple facts or concepts from different domains of memory to arrive at the answer, rather than recalling a single, isolated fact.

# Question Stem Construction
- Frame the stem around a complex, real-world scenario, clinical vignette, or a data interpretation task.
- Ensure the stem presents a complete, stand-alone problem. The student should be able to formulate a potential answer before ever reading the options.
- Consider using the "Item Flipping" technique: present a specific real-world example, patient presentation, or novel outcome in the stem, and have the response options represent the underlying rules, theories, or concepts.
- Avoid negative phrasing (e.g., "Which of the following is NOT...") and double negatives.

# Distractor (Option) Design
- Provide exactly 4 response options (A, B, C, D).
- Never use "All of the above" or "None of the above."
- Ensure all options are roughly the same length, grammatically consistent with the stem, mutually exclusive (no overlapping subsets), and homogenous in content.
- Make all incorrect options (distractors) highly plausible. Base them on actual, common student misconceptions, frequent computational errors, or logical fallacies related to the topic.

# Output Format
- Provide the Question, the Options, the Correct Answer, and a "Rationale" section. In the rationale, explain exactly why the correct answer is the best choice and detail the specific misconception or error in reasoning that each distractor represents.
