# Introduction

The **Vertical Structure of Observed Learning Outcomes (VSOLO)** is a framework built to provide corrective feedback to learners and authors, from beginners to experts. It models progressive levels of understanding.

It's not a tool created to evaluate a person, but the level of development of a work in progress. The discussion if this is or isn't the same process is out of scope for this text.

Levels:
1. Pre-structural
2. Unistructural
3. Multistructural
4. Relational
5. Multirelational
6. Recursive relational



It may also help in writing texts, as sometimes you may able to describe only in one sentence what the text should be about. Or maybe you can't say what you want to write in one sentence, you can only describe the multiple topics that you want the text to approach.



# Levels description

## 1. Pre-structural
**Brief description:** irrelevant ideas; the task wasn't understood

## 2. Unistructural (One sentence)
**Brief description:** one relevant idea

## 3. Multistructural (Multiple sentences; List)
**Brief description:** multiple relevant ideas, but they are disconnected

## 4. Relational (Paragraph)
**Brief description:** multiple relevant ideas connected; a well written paragraph

## 5. Multirelational (Multiple paragraphs)
**Brief description:** a text composed of multiple paragraphs that are at the relational level by themselves, but the connections between them are weak or non-existent

**Example:** In an essay about the causes of the Industrial Revolution, a student at the multirelational level might write one well-structured paragraph about *technological innovations*, another about *economic factors*, and a third about *social changes*. Each paragraph would be at a relational level, with clear connections between the ideas within it. However, the essay might lack a strong overarching thesis that connects these three categories of causes, and the transitions between the paragraphs might be abrupt.


## 6. Recursive relational (Text)
**Brief description:** a text composed of multiple paragraphs in the relational level, but they are connected.

For an text to be classified of the level "Recursive Relational", each of it's parts shall be able to be summarized into a way that it's possible to build a smaller text that condenses the ideas.

# About the SOLO taxonomy
The main inspiration for this work is the the **Structure of Observed Learning Outcomes (SOLO) taxonomy** proposed by John B. Biggs and Kevin F. Collis.

The major critic that I have is about what happens after the **Relational** level. The original model jumps to a level called **Extended abstract**, that can be defined as "The previous integrated whole may be conceptualised at a higher level of abstraction and generalised to a new topic or area", which feels vast and undefined.

While the 'Extended Abstract' level's focus on generalization and application to new domains is valuable for assessing the breadth of a learner's understanding, it does not adequately describe the process of achieving a more profound and integrated mastery within the original area of study."

The "Extended Abstract" level, described in the SOLO taxonomy as the ability to conceptualize at a higher level of abstraction and generalize to new topics, is a valuable cognitive skill. However, this level does not fully capture the process of deepening understanding within a specific domain.

While the previous levels can be used to progress to a deeper learning, building increasingly complex and hierarquical structures within the same topic, the main ideia behind **Extended abstract** is about generalizing to a new topic or area. This departure from the topic being studied makes the original SOLO taxonomy unfit to be used as a guide for a vertical deepening in the study of an area and or production of written works, like essays and books, as it incentivizes exactly tangentiation of the original topic.

That's the main intention behind my proposed changes.
