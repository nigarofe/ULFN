# Proposition

$$
\int \sin(x) \cos(x) dx
$$





# Step-by-step

$$
\int \sin(x) \cos(x) dx
$$

$$
u = \sin(x)
$$

$$
\frac{du}{dx} = \cos(x) \rightarrow dx = \frac{1}{\cos(x)} \cdot du
$$

$$
\int \sin(x) \cos(x) dx = \int u \cdot \cos(x) \cdot \frac{1}{\cos(x)} \cdot du
$$

$$
= \int u du = \frac{u^2}{2} + C = \frac{\sin^2(x)}{2} + C
$$





# Answer

Using $u = \sin(x)$
$= \frac{\sin^2(x)}{2} + C_1$.

Using $u = \cos(x)$
$= -\frac{\cos^2(x)}{2} + C_2$.
