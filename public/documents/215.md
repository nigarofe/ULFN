# Proposition
Find the solution to this system of differential equations:

a) $\begin{cases} y_1' = -y_1 - y_2 \\ y_2' = 2y_1 - 4y_2 \end{cases}$

b) $\begin{cases} y_1' = 3y_1 + 2y_2 \\ y_2' = 4y_1 + y_2 \end{cases}$

Solved items:

- a
- b
- a
- b

# Step-by-step
## a

$$det (A - \lambda I) = 0 \quad (A - \lambda_i I) \vec{v}_i = \vec{0}$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} -1 & -1 \cr 2 & -4 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} \lambda & 0 \cr 0 & \lambda \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1-\lambda & -1 \cr 2 & -4-\lambda \end{bmatrix}$$

$$(-1-\lambda)(-4-\lambda) - (-1)(2)$$

$$4 + \lambda + 4\lambda + \lambda^2 + 2 = 0$$

$$\lambda^2 + 5\lambda + 6 = 0$$

$$\Delta = b^2 - 4ac = 5^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 6 = 1$$

$$\frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\Delta}}{2a} = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{1}}{2 \cdot 1} = \frac{-5 \pm 1}{2} = -2, -3 = \lambda_1, \lambda_2$$

---

$$(A - \lambda_1 I) \vec{v}_1 = \vec{0}$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} -1-(-2) & -1 \cr 2 & -4-(-2) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \cr v_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \cr 0 \end{bmatrix}$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \cr 2 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \cr v_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \cr 0 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow v_1=1, v_2=1$$

$$\vec{v}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cr 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

---

$$(A - \lambda_2 I) \vec{v}_2 = \vec{0}$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} -1-(-3) & -1 \cr 2 & -4-(-3) \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \cr v_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \cr 0 \end{bmatrix}$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \cr 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \cr v_2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \cr 0 \end{bmatrix} \rightarrow v_1=1, v_2=2$$

$$\vec{v}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \cr 2 \end{bmatrix}$$

---

$$\vec{y}(t) = c_1 e^{\lambda_1 t} \vec{v}_1 + c_2 e^{\lambda_2 t} \vec{v}_2$$

$$\begin{bmatrix} y_1(t) \\ y_2(t) \end{bmatrix} = c_1 e^{-2t} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 e^{-3t} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}$$

$$y_1(t) = c_1 e^{-2t} + c_2 e^{-3t}$$

$$y_2(t) = c_1 e^{-2t} + 2c_2 e^{-3t}$$


# Answers
a) $y_1 = c_1 e^{-2t} + c_2 e^{-3t}$ and $y_2 = c_1 e^{-2t} + 2c_2 e^{-3t}$

b) $y_1 = c_1 e^{-t} + c_2 e^{5t}$ and $y_2 = -2 c_1 e^{-t} + c_2 e^{5t}$
