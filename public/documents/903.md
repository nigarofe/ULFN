# Exercise about Duolingo English Test Structure and Scoring

The Duolingo English Test (DET) is a modern language proficiency assessment designed for international students and institutions. Unlike traditional exams, the DET is an adaptive test, meaning the difficulty of the questions adjusts in real-time based on the test-taker's performance. The exam measures four key components: Literacy, Comprehension, Conversation, and Production. It is delivered online and on-demand, utilizing computer vision and AI to ensure testing integrity through remote proctoring.

Scoring on the DET is reported on a scale from 10 to 160, typically presented in 5-point increments. This holistic score is aligned with the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR). For instance, a score of 120 or above generally indicates a high level of proficiency, corresponding to the C1 level. To ensure accuracy, the subscores provide a more granular look at how a candidate handles integrated skills, such as "Literacy," which combines reading and writing abilities rather than testing them in isolation.
