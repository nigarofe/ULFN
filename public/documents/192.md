# Proposition
Solve the following differential equations:

a) $y''' + 7y'' + 14y' + 8y = 0$, where $y(0) = 3$, $y'(0) = -7$ and $y''(0) = 14$
b) $y''' + 8y'' + 19y' + 12y = 0$, where $y(0) = 4$, $y'(0) = -12$, $y''(0) = 42$
c) $y''' + 9y'' + 26y' + 24y = 0$, where $y(0) = 5$, $y'(0) = -16$, $y''(0) = 54$
d) $y''' + 10y'' + 31y' + 30y = 0$, where $y(0) = 6$, $y'(0) = -19$, $y''(0) = 71$
e) $y''' + 11y'' + 36y' + 36y = 0$, where $y(0) = 5$, $y'(0) = -17$, $y''(0) = 67$
f) $y''' + 10y'' + 29y' + 20y = 0$, where $y(0) = 7$, $y'(0) = -30$, $y''(0) = 142$

Solved items: 

- a
- b
- c
- d

## a
$$y''' + 7y'' + 14y' + 8y = 0$$
$$ y(0) = 3 \quad y'(0) = -7 \quad  y''(0) = 14$$
$$r^3 + 7r^2 + 14r + 8 = 0$$

**Rational Root Theorem**
Possible roots are factors of $8$ ($\pm 1, \pm 2, \pm 4, \pm 8$). Checking these values, we find that $r = -1$ is a root, because:

$$(-1)^3 + 7(-1)^2 + 14(-1) + 8 = -1 + 7 - 14 + 8 = 0$$

**Synthetic Division**
Using synthetic division with $r = -1$:
$$
\begin{array}{c|cccc}
  & 1 & 7 & 14 & 8 \\
-1 &   & -1 & -6 & -8 \\
\hline
  & 1 & 6 & 8 & 0 \\
\end{array}
$$

This gives us the factorization:
$$r^3 + 7r^2 + 14r + 8 = (r + 1)(r^2 + 6r + 8)$$

**Solving the Quadratic**
The quadratic $r^2 + 6r + 8 = 0$ can be factored as:
$$(r + 2)(r + 4) = 0$$

---

So the roots are $r_1 = -1, r_2 = -2, r_3 = -4$.

---

$$y = c_1 e^{r_1x} + c_2 e^{r_2x} + c_3 e^{r_3x}$$
$$y = c_1 e^{-x} + c_2 e^{-2x} + c_3 e^{-4x}$$
$$y' = -c_1 e^{-x} - 2c_2 e^{-2x} - 4c_3 e^{-4x}$$
$$y'' = c_1 e^{-x} + 4c_2 e^{-2x} + 16c_3 e^{-4x}$$



**Applying Initial Conditions**
$$y(0) = c_1 + c_2 + c_3 = 3$$
$$y'(0) = -c_1 - 2c_2 - 4c_3 = -7$$
$$y''(0) = c_1 + 4c_2 + 16c_3 = 14$$


**Gaussian Elimination**
$$
\left[
\begin{array}{ccc|r}
  1 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\
  -1 & -2 & -4 & -7 \\
  1 & 4 & 16 & 14 \\
\end{array}
\right]
$$
$$\text{Row 3 += Row 2} \quad \text{Row 2 += Row 1}$$
$$
\left[
\begin{array}{ccc|r}
  1 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\
  0 & -1 & -3 & -4 \\
  0 & 2 & 12 & 7 \\
\end{array}
\right]
$$
$$\text{Row 3 += 2 Row 2}$$
$$
\left[
\begin{array}{ccc|r}
  1 & 1 & 1 & 3 \\
  0 & -1 & -3 & -4 \\
  0 & 0 & 6 & -1 \\
\end{array}
\right]
$$
$$\implies c_3 = -\frac{1}{6} \implies c_2 = \frac{9}{2} \implies c_1 = - \frac{4}{3}$$
$$y(x) = -\frac{4}{3}e^{-x} + \frac{9}{2}e^{-2x} - \frac{1}{6}e^{-4x}$$
