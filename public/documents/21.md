# Proposition

$$
\int x^3 \ln(x) dx
$$





# Step-by-step

$$
\int x^3 \ln(x) dx
$$

$$
u = \ln(x) \quad u' = 1/x
$$

$$
v' = x^3 \quad v = x^4/4
$$

$$
\int uv' = uv - \int u'v
$$

$$
\ln(x) \cdot \frac{x^4}{4} - \int 1/x \cdot \frac{x^4}{4}dx
$$

$$
\ln(x) \cdot \frac{x^4}{4} - 1/4 \int x^3 dx
$$

$$
\frac{x^4}{4} \ln(x) - 1/4 \cdot \frac{x^4}{4} + C
$$

$$
\frac{x^4}{4} (\ln(x) - 1/4) + C
$$





# Answer

$$
\frac{x^4}{4} (\ln(x) - 1/4) + C
$$
