# Proposition
Solve these differential equations by separating them:

a) $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2}{y(1+x^3)}$
b) $\frac{dy}{dx} - y^2 \sin(x) = 0$
c) $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{4x^3-1}{y}$
d) $x \frac{dy}{dx} = (1-y^2)^{1/2}$
e) $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{(5x^4-1)}{y^2}$
f) $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x^2}{1+y^4}$

Solved items:
- a, b, c, d, e, f
- a, b
- c, d


# Step-by-step
## d
$$
\int \frac{1}{\sqrt{a^2-x^2}} \, dx = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{u}{a}\right) + c
$$

---

$$
x \frac{dy}{dx} = (1-y^2)^{1/2}
$$
$$
x \frac{1}{dx} = (1-y^2)^{1/2} \frac{1}{dy}
$$
$$
\frac{1}{x} dx = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-y^2}} dy
$$
$$
\int \frac{1}{x} dx = \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-y^2}} dy
$$
$$
\ln|x| + C = \sin^{-1}(y)
$$
$$
y = \sin(\ln|x| + C)
$$

# Answer
a) $y = ((2/3)\ln|1 + x^3| + C)^{1/2}$
b) $y = 1 / (C - cos(x))$
c) $y = (2x^4 - 2x + C)^{1/2}$
d) $y = \sin(\ln|x| + C)$
e) $y = (3x^5 - 3x + C)^{1/3}$
f) $15y + 3y^5 = 5x^3 + C$
