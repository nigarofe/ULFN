# Proposition
Solve for y in these differential equations
a) $\frac{dy}{dx} - x^3 = 0$, where $y(0) = 5$

b) $\frac{dy}{dx} - \cos(x) = 0$, where $y(0) = 1$

c) $y \frac{dy}{dx} - x = 0$, where $y(0) = 0$

d) $\frac{dy}{dx} - x^3 - x^4 = 0$, where $y(0) = 0$

e) $\cos(y) \frac{dy}{dx} - x = 0$, where $y(0) = 0$

f) $e^y \frac{dy}{dx} -2x = 0$, where $y(0) = \ln(2)$

Solved items: 
- a, b, c, d, e, f
- a, b, c
- d, e, f

# Step-by-step
## a
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} - x^3 = 0
$$
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} = x^3
$$
$$
dy = x^3 \, dx
$$
$$
\int \, dy = \int x^3 \, dx
$$
$$
y = \frac{x^4}{4} + C
$$
$$
y(0) = 5 \implies y = \frac{x^4}{4} + 5
$$

## b
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} - \cos(x) = 0
$$
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} = \cos(x)
$$
$$
dy = \cos(x) \, dx
$$
$$
\int \, dy = \int \cos(x) \, dx
$$
$$
y = \sin(x) + C
$$
$$
y(0) = 1 \implies y = \sin(x) + 1
$$

## c
$$
y \frac{dy}{dx} - x = 0
$$
$$
y \frac{dy}{dx} = x
$$
$$
y \, dy = x \, dx
$$
$$
\int y \, dy = \int x \, dx
$$
$$
\frac{y^2}{2} = \frac{x^2}{2} + C
$$
$$
y^2 = x^2 + C
$$
$$
y(0) = 0 \implies y^2 = x^2 \implies y = \pm x
$$

## d
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} - x^3 - x^4 = 0
$$
$$
\frac{dy}{dx} = x^3 + x^4
$$
$$
dy = (x^3 + x^4) \, dx
$$
$$
\int \, dy = \int (x^3 + x^4) \, dx
$$
$$
y = \frac{x^4}{4} + \frac{x^5}{5} + C
$$
$$
y(0) = 0 \implies y = \frac{x^4}{4} + \frac{x^5}{5}
$$

## e
$$
\cos(y) \frac{dy}{dx} - x = 0
$$
$$
\cos(y) \frac{dy}{dx} = x
$$
$$
\cos(y) \, dy = x \, dx
$$
$$
\int \cos(y) \, dy = \int x \, dx
$$
$$
\sin(y) = \frac{x^2}{2} + C
$$
$$
y(0) = 0 \implies \sin(y) = \frac{x^2}{2}
$$
$$
y = \arcsin\left(\frac{x^2}{2}\right)
$$

## f
$$
e^y \frac{dy}{dx} - 2x = 0
$$
$$
e^y \frac{dy}{dx} = 2x
$$
$$
e^y \, dy = 2x \, dx
$$
$$
\int e^y \, dy = \int 2x \, dx
$$
$$
e^y = x^2 + C
$$
$$
y = \ln(x^2 + C)
$$
$$
y(0) = \ln(2) \implies y = \ln(x^2 + 2)
$$

# Answer
a) $y = \frac{x^4}{4} + 5$
b) $y = \sin(x) + 1$
c) $y = \pm x$
d) $y = \frac{x^4}{4} + \frac{x^5}{5}$
e) $y = \arcsin\left(\frac{x^2}{2}\right)$
f) $y = \ln(x^2 + 2)$
