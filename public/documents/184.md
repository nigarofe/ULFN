# Proposition
Solve the following exact differential equations:

a) $3x^2 + 2y \frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
b) $\frac{1}{y^2} - \frac{2x}{y^3} \frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
c) $y + 2x + x \frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
d) $\frac{1}{y} - \frac{x}{y^2} \frac{dy}{dx} = 0$

Solved items:
- a
- a, b, c, d
- a, b
- c, d
- a, b
- c, d

# Step-by-step
## General solution (assuming the equation is exact)
$$
M(x,y) + N(x,y) \frac{dy}{dx} = 0
$$

---

$$
\text{The Union Method}
$$
1. Calculate $\int M \, dx$.
2. Calculate $\int N \, dy$.
3. Write down every distinct term you see exactly once.

$$
f(x,y) = \int M(x,y) \, dx \, \cup\,  \int N(x,y) \, dy = C
$$

**Observation:** This is not standard mathematical notation, it's a mnemonic device.

---

$$
\text{Method from first principles}
$$

$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial x} f(x,y) = M(x,y) \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial y} f(x,y) = N(x,y)
$$
$$
\int \frac{\partial}{\partial x} f(x,y)\, dx = \int M(x,y) \, dx
$$
$$
{\color{red}f(x,y)} = \int M(x,y) \, dx = {\color{green} g(x,y) + h(y)}
$$

---

$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial y} {\color{red}f(x,y)} = N(x,y)
$$
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial y} {\color{green} \left[g(x,y) + h(y)\right]} = N(x,y)
$$
$$
g'(x,y) + h'(y) = N(x,y)
$$
$$
h'(y) = N(x,y) - g'(x,y)
$$
$$
\int h'(y) \, dy = \int N(x,y) - g'(x,y) \, dy
$$
$$
h(y) = \int N(x,y) \, dy - \int g'(x,y) \, dy
$$

---

$$
f(x,y) = \int M(x,y) \, dx + h(y)
$$


$$
f(x,y) = \int M(x,y) \, dx + \int \left[ N(x,y) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \left( \int M(x,y) \, dx \right) \right] \, dy = C
$$


## a
$$
3x^2 + 2y \frac{dy}{dx} = 0
$$
$$
M(x,y) + N(x,y) \frac{dy}{dx} = 0
$$
$$
M(x,y) = 3x^2 \quad N(x,y) = 2y
$$

### From the Union Method
$$
\int M \, dx = \int 3x^2 \, dx = x^3
$$
$$
\int N \, dy = \int 2y \, dy = y^2
$$
$$
f(x,y) = \int M(x,y) \, dx \, \cup\,  \int N(x,y) \, dy = C
$$
$$
f(x,y) = x^3 + y^2 = c
$$
$$
y = (c-x^3)^{1/2}
$$

### From first principles
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial x} f(x,y) = M(x,y) \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial y} f(x,y) = N(x,y)
$$
$$
\int \frac{\partial}{\partial x} f(x,y)\, dx = \int M(x,y) \, dx
$$
$$
f(x,y) = \int 3x^2 \, dx + h(y) = x^3 + h(y)
$$
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial y} f(x,y) = N(x,y)
$$
$$
\frac{\partial}{\partial y} \left[g(x,y) + h(y)\right] = N(x,y)
$$
$$
h'(y) = N(x,y) - g'(x,y)
$$
$$
h(y) = \int N(x,y) \, dy - \int g'(x,y) \, dy
$$
$$
h(y) = \int 2y \, dy - \int \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (x^3) \, dy = y^2
$$
$$
h(y) = \int 2y \, dy - \int 0 \, dy = y^2
$$
$$
f(x,y) = x^3 + y^2 = c
$$
$$
c = x^3 + y^2 \implies y = (c-x^3)^{1/2}
$$


# Answer
a) $y = (c-x^3)^{1/2}$
b) $y = (cx)^{1/2}$
c) $y = \frac{(c-x^2)}{x}$
d) $y = \frac{x}{c} = cx$

**Observation:** The solution for d) in the book ($y = \frac{c}{x}$) is incorrect.
