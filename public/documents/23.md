# Proposition

$$
\int \sin^2(x)dx
$$





# Step-by-step

$$
\cos(2\theta) = 2\cos^2(\theta)-1
$$

$$
= 1-2\sin^2(\theta)
$$

$$
\sin^2(\theta) = - \frac{\cos(2\theta)-1}{2}
$$

---

$$
\int \sin^2(x)dx =  -1/2 \int \cos(2x)dx + 1/2 \int dx
$$

---

$$
u=2x
$$

$$
\frac{du}{dx}=2 \implies dx = 1/2 du
$$

---

$$
= -1/2 \cdot 1/2 \int \cos u \,du + 1/2 x
$$

$$
-1/4 \sin(2x) + 1/2 x
$$





# Answer

$$
-1/4 \sin(2x) + 1/2 x
$$
