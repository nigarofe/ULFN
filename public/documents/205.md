# Proposition
Are the singular points of the following differential equations regular or irregular?

a) $x \frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} + \frac{y}{x} = 0$

b) $\frac{d^2 y}{dx^2} + \frac{y}{(x^2-4)} = 0$

# Step-by-step
If $\lim_{x \to x_0} (x - x_0) p(x)$ and $\lim_{x \to x_0} (x - x_0)^2 q(x)$ exist and are finite, then $x_0$ is a regular singular point.

# Answers
a) The singular points are regular.

b) The singular points are regular.
