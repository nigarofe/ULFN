# Proposition

$$
\int \ln(x) \, dx
$$





# Step-by-step

$$
\frac{d}{dx} \ln(x) = \frac{1}{x} = \frac{d}{dx} \int x^{-1} \, dx
$$

$$
\int u \, v' = u \, v - \int u' \, v
$$

$$
u = \ln(x) \quad v' = 1
$$

$$
u' = \frac{1}{x} \quad v = x
$$

$$
\int \ln(x) \, dx = \ln(x) \cdot x - \int \frac{1}{x} \cdot x \, dx
$$

 

$$
= x \ln(x) - x + C
$$





# Answer

$$
x \ln(x) - x + C
$$
