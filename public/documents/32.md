# Proposition

$$
\int \frac{1}{4y-8}\,dy
$$





# Step-by-step

$$
\int \frac{1}{4y-8}\,dy
$$

$$
u = 4y - 8
$$

$$
\frac{du}{dy} = 4 \implies dy = \frac{1}{4}du
$$

$$
\int \frac{1}{u}\,\frac{1}{4} du = \frac{1}{4}\int u^{-1}\,du = \frac{1}{4}\ln u + C = \frac{1}{4}\ln(4y-8) + C
$$





# Answer

$$
\frac{1}{4}\ln|4y - 8| + c = \frac{1}{4}\ln|y - 2| + c'
$$
