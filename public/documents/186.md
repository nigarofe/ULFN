# Proposition
Solve the following differential equations:

a) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 4 \frac{dy}{dx} + 3y = 0$, where $y(0) = 2$ and $y'(0) = -4$.

b) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 5 \frac{dy}{dx} + 6y = 0$, where $y(0) = 2$ and $y'(0) = -5$.

c) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 5 \frac{dy}{dx} + 6y = 0$, where $y(0) = 3$ and $y'(0) = -8$.

d) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 6 \frac{dy}{dx} + 8y = 0$, where $y(0) = 4$ and $y'(0) = -14$.

e) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 6 \frac{dy}{dx} + 5y = 0$, where $y(0) = 3$ and $y'(0) = -11$.

f) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 7 \frac{dy}{dx} + 6y = 0$, where $y(0) = 5$ and $y'(0) = -25$.

Solved items: 
- a, b
- c, d
- e
- f


# Step-by-step
## a
$$
y = c_1e^{r_1x} + c_2e^{r_2x}
$$
$$
\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 4 \frac{dy}{dx} + 3y = 0 \quad y(0) = 2 \quad y'(0) = -4
$$
$$
r^2 + 4r + 3 = 0
$$
$$
\Delta = 4^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 3 = 4
$$
$$
r_1, r_2 = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\Delta}}{2a} = \frac{-4 \pm 2}{2} = -1, -3
$$
$$
y = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{-3x}
$$
$$
y' = ...
$$

---

$$
\frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)
$$
$$
f = e^x \quad f' = e^x
$$
$$
g = -x \quad g' = -1
$$
$$
\frac{d}{dx} c_1e^{-x} = c_1 (e^{-x} \cdot(-1)) = -c_1e^{-x}
$$

---

$$
y' = -c_1e^{-x} - 3c_2e^{-3x}
$$
$$
y(0) = c_1 + c_2 = 2
$$
$$
y'(0) = -c_1 - 3c_2 = -4
$$
$$
\begin{cases}
c_1 + c_2 = 2 \\
-c_1 - 3c_2 = -4
\end{cases}
$$
$$
\implies -2c_2 = -2 \implies c_2 = 1 \implies c_1 = 1
$$
$$
y = e^{-x} + e^{-3x}
$$

## d (with Gaussian elimination / augmented matrix)
$$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + 6 \frac{dy}{dx} + 8y = 0 \quad y(0) = 4 \quad y'(0) = -14$$
$$y = c_1e^{r_1x} + c_2e^{r_2x}$$
$$r^2 + 6r + 8 = 0$$
$$\Delta = b^2 - 4ac = 6^2 - 4 \cdot 1 \cdot 8 = 4$$
$$r_1, r_2 = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{\Delta}}{2a} = \frac{-6 \pm 2}{2} = -2, -4$$
$$y = c_1e^{-2x} + c_2e^{-4x}$$
$$y' = -2c_1e^{-2x} - 4c_2e^{-4x}$$
$$y(0) = c_1 + c_2 = 4$$
$$y'(0) = -2c_1 - 4c_2 = -14$$
$$
\left[
\begin{array}{cc|r}
  1 & 1 & 4 \\
  -2 & -4 & -14
\end{array}
\right]

\implies

\left[
\begin{array}{cc|r}
  1 & 1 & 4 \\
  -1 & -2 & -7
\end{array}
\right]

\implies
\left[
\begin{array}{cc|r}
0 & -1 & -3
\end{array}
\right]
$$
$$
\implies c_2 = 3 \implies c_1 = 1
$$
